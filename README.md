# Arch_Setup
This is a simple tutorial on how to install Arch Linux efficently and with good quality.

  1. Firstly, get a bootable usb stick and install the Arch Linux iso. After (if your in windows) go to your advance restart then click enter. Wait for it to load then enter into the devices catagorie, you should then see your usb stick and enter into it.
  2. Now you'll boot into Arch Linux setup and now this is where you'll have to type `iwctl` to configure your WiFi. after you type `iwctl`, proceed to type `station (your connection type e.g. wlan0) connect (SSID)` then hit enter, it'll then ask you to enter in your passphrase which is just your password.
  3. After connecting to your WiFi you can exit iwctl then type `pacman -S archlinux-keyring` as you'll need this to install your OS later on. Then type `archinstall`, this is where you'll configure your actuall OS and how your prefrences will be. You firstly enter the language of your need then enter into Mirrors, select manual and find your country in the list. Mirrors will help when your updating your system at time and will update near the closet Mirror system so you can update quicker. After head into Driver(s), this is where your drive(s) will be allocated for space and which drive will hold the OS. Configure it to your liking base on your drive (e.g. BTRFS for nvme filesystem). Bootloader is the next section, this is where the users sees what OS is available to them and this can be set to look goo-ey or minimal. If your looking into a nice customizable bootloader then I suggest to get Grub, if not, then get systemd-boot. Next choose your Hostname to anything you want, this will be the computers name. This next part is very important to remember as you'll be setting up your root password which is the most powerful command to run anything, make this password into something you'll remember. Up next is the User account, this is where you can add users or add a user like yourself, make sure to enable sudo for your user with a good name. Profile is where you'll make your login page to your OS look easier nice or minimalist. I suggest to get sddm as its very customizable to make it look nice towards your prefrence, if not and you want it to look simple, use ly. For audio enable bluetooth and also pipewire as their the newest forms of audio for Arch Linux and are very much recommended. For your Kernels, these are entirely up to you and what you want your hardware to do, I personally got the Linux and Linux-zen kernels as they fit my needs. For Network-Manager enable it as their so much easier to set up befeorehand than in your already installed Arch Linux where you need to peform extra steps to install it w/out WiFi. Now for the final step of archinstall, click Install. Wait for some time before the installation is finalized and you can reboot into Arch Linux.
   4. After you reboot, you'll be greeted by a login page of the one you configured in the profile section. Enter in your password then you'll enter you Arch Linux install! After this point you can do anything now. !DISCLAIMER! Make sure to run `sudo pacman -Syu` instantly after installation.
